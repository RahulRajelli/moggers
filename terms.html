What the checker actually measures

ATS X-Ray reads the text layer of your PDF and reports what a parser can extract from it: whether text exists, whether ligature glyphs have made words unsearchable, page count, text density and whether contact details are machine-readable.

It is not a real applicant tracking system, and it is not connected to any employer's. Different ATS products parse differently. A clean result here means the common, silent failure modes are absent — it does not mean any particular employer's system will read your resume perfectly, and it says nothing about whether your resume is any good. Keyword coverage against a job description is a heuristic proxy, not a score anyone hiring will ever see.
